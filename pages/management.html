Technical Management

Management in the technology sector involves overseeing the development, launch, and continuous improvement of tech products. This role bridges the gap between business objectives, user needs, and technical capabilities, ensuring that products deliver value to customers and align with the company's strategic goals.

Jobs in This Field

Technical Product Manager

Focuses on products with a strong technical component, working closely with engineering teams.

Skills Required

Deep technical knowledge, understanding of software architecture, and technical problem-solving.

Growth Product Manager

Focuses on strategies to drive user acquisition, engagement, retention, and monetization.

Skills Required:

Data analysis, A/B testing, marketing strategies, and growth hacking techniques.

Solutions Architect

Designs and implements technology solutions to address specific business problems.

Skills Required:

Deep technical knowledge (cloud computing, programming languages), problem-solving, communication, understanding of business processes

Information Architect

Organizes and structures information to make it easily discoverable and usable for users. They often collaborate with UX designers and content creators.

Skills Required:

User research, information organization, usability testing, content strategy, understanding of user behavior.

Quality Assurance Testers

A QA tester is someone who works with software or a website to ensure it runs properly. They try to prevent faulty apps or other technology from getting to the customer. This is a highly growing site.

Skills Required:

Basic programming skills, Agile testing, knowledge of Software Development Life Cycle, Cross-platform Testing skills, testing tools and techniques like blackbox testing, UI testing, functional testing. Additionally automation testing tools like Selenium, Appium, and Cypress.

Engineering Manager

Engineering managers coordinate project activities, oversee budgets and schedules, and ensure the team's work is delivered on time, meets client expectations and adheres to quality standards.

Skills Required:

Technical Knowledge familiarity with some coding languages and frameworks, Business knowledge, Project management, problem solving, people skills.

UI Designer

A User Interface Designer is responsible for designing the visual elements of the product. Implementing visual design principles like color theory, typography, and layout.

Skills Required:

Expertise in design principles, proficiency in using tools like Figma, Adobe XD, sketch.

UX Designer

A User Experience Designer is responsible for Researching user needs and behavior. Creating wireframes, prototypes, and user flows to optimize usability.

Skills Required:

Same as a UI Designer, along with effective communication skills.

Software Architect

A Software Architect designs and builds the technical blueprint for complex software systems

Skills Required:

Deep technical knowledge on cloud computing and of programming languages and strong leadership skills to guide development teams and ensure project’s architectural vision is realised.

Job Duties:

Product Vision and Strategy

Strategic Leadership

Defining and articulating a compelling product vision that aligns with the company's overall goals and drives product development

Market Research

Customer Insights and Competitive Analysis

Conducting market research to identify customer needs, pain points, and market trends, as well as analyzing competitors to gain a competitive advantage.

Requirement Gathering

User-Centric Product Definition

Translating business and user needs into clear, actionable product requirements and user stories that guide development.

Cross-functional Collaboration

Team Leadership and Coordination

Working closely with engineering, design, marketing, sales, and support teams to ensure effective collaboration, alignment, and successful product delivery.

Product Development

Lifecycle Management

Overseeing the product development process from inception to launch, ensuring adherence to timelines, managing trade-offs, and making strategic decisions.

Product Testing and Defect Management

Quality Assurance

Collaborating with QA testers to ensure rigorous testing, identify and address defects, and deliver a high-quality product.

Product Launch

Go-to-Market Strategy

Planning and executing successful product launches, including marketing campaigns, sales enablement, and user training.

Performance Monitoring

Continuous Improvement

Tracking product performance metrics, gathering user feedback, and iterating on the product to enhance user satisfaction, drive adoption, and achieve business objectives.

How to Become One

Qualification Details Courses in Somaiya Technical Skills Development Tools Certifications Soft Skills
  • Education: A bachelor's degree in computer science is a strong foundation.
  • Core Skills: Hone your data analysis, problem-solving, and Agile methodology understanding.
  • Product-Specific Skills: Consider building a portfolio showcasing basic prototyping and understanding of A/B testing.
  • Functional Roles: Tailor your resume to highlight relevant technical skills like programming languages, marketing tools, or design software depending on your target function (engineering, marketing, design).
Both product and functional fields need strong data analysis and Agile knowledge. Product folks benefit from prototyping, A/B testing, and basic coding for communication. Functional roles like engineering, marketing, and design require specific technical skills in their domains (programming languages, marketing tools, design software). Focus on core skills and research the specifics for your desired role. Many online resources can help you develop these in-demand abilities.
The development tools used in product and functional fields will depend on the specific role, but here's a general breakdown:
  • Common Tools Across Fields:
    • Project Management: Tools like Jira, Asana, or Monday.com help manage tasks, track progress, and collaborate within teams.
    • Communication: Slack or Microsoft Teams are popular for quick communication and information sharing.
    • Version Control: Git is a version control system for tracking changes in code or other files.
  • Product Management:
    • Prototyping: Figma, Balsamiq, or InVision allow creating mockups and prototypes to visualize product ideas.
    • Product Roadmapping: Tools like ProductPlan or Aha! help visualize and manage product roadmaps.
    • User Research/Testing: User testing tools can help gather feedback on prototypes and designs.
Relevant Industry certifications are a plus. Certifications can be online or offline. Online Certificates need to be from verified sources like:
  1. Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM) certification
  2. For UI/UX:
    • Google UX Design Professional Certificate
    • Google UI Design Certification
  3. For QA Testers
    • Certified Software Testing Engineer
    • Certified Software Quality Analyst (CSQA)
  4. For Solutions and Information Architecture
    • AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate Certification
    • Microsoft Certified: Azure Solutions Architect Expert
  • Communication: Being able to clearly and concisely communicate ideas, both verbally and in writing, is essential for collaborating effectively with diverse teams.
  • Collaboration: Working seamlessly within teams and across departments is crucial. This requires active listening, empathy, and the ability to build strong relationships.
  • Problem-solving: Identifying problems, analyzing situations, and coming up with creative solutions are important aspects of these fields.
  • Adaptability: The tech industry is constantly evolving, so being adaptable and open to learning new tools and technologies is key.
  • Leadership: Product managers often need to lead cross-functional teams, so strong leadership skills are essential.
